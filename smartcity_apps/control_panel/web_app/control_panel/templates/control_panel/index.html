Kubernetes-native Smart City Management

OpenCityHub is a smart city management system built on Kubernetes, ensuring scalability, cost-efficiency, and fault tolerance. Leveraging Kubernetes-native architecture, this platform adapts seamlessly to the dynamic and evolving needs of a modern city.

Traffic Management

Optimize traffic flow, reduce congestion, and enhance public safety using advanced face recognition and predictive algorithms, streamlining license management for a more efficient urban transportation system.

Climate Management

Stay informed about weather conditions with real-time forecasts, process IoT data, and proactively alert citizens to ensure their safety, contributing to a resilient and climate-conscious urban environment.

Pollution Management

Source pollution data effectively and send timely alerts, fostering a healthier environment for the community and promoting sustainable practices through informed decision-making.

Health Emergency Planning

Monitor hospital resources, plan efficient ambulance routes, and provide immediate alerts during health emergencies, prioritizing citizen safety and supporting effective emergency response efforts.

Public Safety Management

Enhance public safety with panic buttons, optimized police route planning, and quick activation of safety protocols during incidents like fires or terror attacks, ensuring a secure urban environment.

Public Events Management

Forecast and manage public events for smooth crowd control and safety, elevating the overall experience for attendees while efficiently implementing safety protocols.

Emergency Incident Management

Prioritize and manage emergency incidents seamlessly by coordinating efforts across police, fire, traffic, ambulance, and hospitals, ensuring swift and effective responses for community safety.

E-Governance

Efficiently handle citizen complaints and provide services through a dedicated Citizen Service Center, promoting transparency and citizen engagement for enhanced overall governance.

Energy Management

Forecast energy demand, implement smart city lighting, and analyze building energy patterns for a sustainable and efficient energy ecosystem, contributing to a greener and more resource-conscious urban landscape.
